This folder is the output of **Process A**: from the single submitted prompt in
[`../prompts/prompt-narrative.md`](../prompts/prompt-narrative.md), Claude Code
generated four unique, comprehensive sub-prompts that **Process B** then runs in
sequence to grow the narrative review from scaffold to publication quality. The
method is adapted from the author's prior single-prompt build
(`single-prompt-bill/auto-bill-02` and its `sub-prompts/`), re-targeted to the
`kevinkawchak/law-physical-ai-trials` repository, the `review/` tree, and the
eight emotional-pillar narrative defined by the prompt's SECTION GOALS.

## Convention

Every sub-prompt opens with a `## prompt-<name>` heading followed by the prompt
text, mirroring the prior repository. Each stage that runs a sub-prompt writes a
paired `output-*.md` narrative file, and the three narrative stages additionally
re-file their own `prompt-*.md` verbatim inside their directory.

## What is unique to this build (the adaptation)

1. **Target.** Every prompt targets `kevinkawchak/law-physical-ai-trials`,
   `review/`, and the transition of H. R. 9510 **v5.0** into Federal law;
   repository release **v0.1.0**.
2. **Subject.** The objective is an emotional-yet-evidenced narrative review with
   eight body sections (compassion, fear of preventable harm, moral outrage,
   hope, responsibility and duty, protection of vulnerable people, trust and
   reassurance, urgency), each pairing a felt appeal with a cited fact.
3. **Media.** Colored Mermaid (native in Markdown, reproduced as colored TikZ in
   the compiled LaTeX) plus full-width tables and ASCII, all in the strict
   palette black, grayscales, `#EBCB8B`, `#D08770`, `#8B2E3F`. No raster images.
4. **Self-learning.** The draft leaves bracketed instructions naming exact source
   files; the full stage processes them into prose; the final stage applies the
   corrections identified from the full stage. The template
   (`../template`) is reused as is and is not regenerated by a sub-prompt.

## Self-learning note

The sub-prompts encode the formatting lessons carried forward from the author's
prior builds: the `>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{<width>}` table column
convention, the `\clearpage` / `\vspace` / `\hspace` page balancing, maximal
widow and club penalties so no line is stranded, the single-hyphen and
section-symbol ("§") rules, and the colored TikZ figure primitive that reproduces
each Mermaid diagram natively in LaTeX without a raster image.
